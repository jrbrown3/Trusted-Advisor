Welcome to Trusted Advisor

This system is built around a single framework: trust is not a feeling — it's a measurable, buildable asset.

The next few screens explain the model that powers everything here. Understanding it will make every feature click.

The Trust Equation

Developed by David Maister, the Trust Equation breaks down exactly what creates — and destroys — trust with another person:

Trust = (Credibility + Reliability + Intimacy) ÷ Self-Orientation

Three things in the numerator build trust. One thing in the denominator — your perceived agenda — can wipe it all out. The denominator is the silent deal-killer in senior relationships.

Building the numerator

The three trust-builders each require a different discipline:

C
Credibility — do they trust what you say? Built through domain expertise, relevant insight, and intellectual honesty.
R
Reliability — do they trust what you do? Built through consistently doing exactly what you said you would, every time.
I
Intimacy — do they trust you with what matters? Built by creating psychological safety and showing you can hold sensitive information well.

The denominator: Self-Orientation

Self-Orientation is the silent deal-killer. It's the degree to which the other person believes your motives are about you — your deal, your quota, your agenda — rather than genuinely about them.

You can have perfect credibility, bulletproof reliability, and real intimacy — and a single interaction where you clearly want something from them will halve your trust score.

The antidote: give before you ask, every time. Consistent insight delivery (tracked in this app) is how you keep self-orientation low over a portfolio of relationships.

The four discovery layers

Every relationship exists at a depth. Most advisors stay at Layer 1 and wonder why they can't get traction. The real leverage is at 3 and 4.

1
Surface — Role, org structure, stated priorities. Where everyone starts.
2
Operational — Real KPIs, team pressures, budget authority, what's actually on fire.
3
Strategic — Long-term agenda, board pressures, competitive fears, where they're taking the org.
4
Personal — Career motivations, legacy intent, risk tolerance, what keeps them up at night.
